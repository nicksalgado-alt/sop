# Lessons Learned

## Smart Locks

- Functional reset and accessory completeness can materially change inspection and refurb time.
- Defective-unit parts usage is a high-variance driver and should be asked early.

## Small Electronics (Earbuds)

- Small form factor benefits from labor time adjustments *down* from global benchmarks (picking, packing, refurbishment touches are quicker).
- Example: Refurbish sellable-used drops from 12 min to 10 min; pick-pack parcel drops from 4 min to 3 min.
- Defective-unit parts cost is highest uncertainty and should be validated early via actual defect breakdown (battery vs. driver vs. Bluetooth vs. water damage).
- Accessory completeness materially affects refurbishment time; ask early if charging case and cables are typically included.

## Smart Locks (Rate Card Specifics)

- At $35/hr labor, the blended unit cost for smart locks comes to ~$10.19 — refurb path dominates at $15.76/unit.
- Smart locks have higher NTF rate (~25%) than typical electronics because of installation-related returns (wrong door fit, deadbolt incompatibility).
- Firmware reset and previous-owner pairing data clearance is a critical refurb step — must factory reset every unit.
- Motor/deadbolt cycling test (10x cycle) is important QA step; motor weakness = route to defective.
- Smart lock return rate estimated at ~10% (between ecommerce avg ~20% and manufacturer defect rate ~1-2%).
- Shipping cost per resale unit is higher ($12) due to weight (~2-3 lbs) vs small electronics.
- The NextSense Rate Card & SOP format works well as a template for all product categories.

## General

- A fast first-pass estimate is usually better than over-interviewing at the start.
- Clear benchmark labeling builds trust when exact numbers are not available.
