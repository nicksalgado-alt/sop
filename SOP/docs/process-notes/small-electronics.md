# Small Electronics (Earbuds, Charging Accessories, etc.)

## Category Notes

Small consumer electronics like Bluetooth earbuds have unique processing characteristics compared to the global benchmarks.

### Key Traits

- **Small form factor** → faster picking, packing, and physical handling
- **Accessory completeness** → varies widely (case, cables, documentation may or may not be present)
- **Defect types** → often battery, driver, Bluetooth pairing, or water damage (specific repair costs vary)
- **Light weight** → packaging and dunnage costs lower than larger devices

### Time Adjustments from Global Benchmarks

| Step | Global | Small Elec. | Notes |
|------|--------|---------|-------|
| Refurbish as sellable used | 12 min | 10 min | Smaller device, quicker cleaning and reset |
| Pick and pack parcel | 4 min | 3 min | Compact packaging, less handling |
| Pick and pack bulk | 2 min | 1.5–2 min | Quick carton/tray placement |

### Parts Cost Adjustments

- **Refurbish sellable used:** Reduce from $3.50 to $2.50 (fewer consumables needed)
- **Refurbish defective:** Reduce from $7.00 to $5.00 (smaller parts, fewer replacements; validate against actual defect breakdown)
- **Pick and pack parcel:** Reduce packaging/handling from $1.60 to $1.00 (minimal dunnage)

### Common Defect Patterns

Based on initial Bluetooth earbud estimate:
- **Battery dead** (estimated 40% of defective): $2–$3 per unit to replace
- **Driver failed** (estimated 30%): $6–$8 per unit to repair
- **Bluetooth pairing/connection** (estimated 20%): $1–$2 (reset, reflash firmware)
- **Water/moisture damage** (estimated 10%): often unsalvageable, scrap cost only

*Recommendation:* Validate this mix early on real sample of returned units.

### Accessory Handling

Ask early:
- Do most units include charging case, charging cable, and documentation?
- If accessories are missing or damaged, add time and cost for sourcing/bundling replacements.
- If 20%+ of units are missing accessories, refurbish time can extend by 1–2 minutes per unit.

### Fulfillment Mode

Small electronics typically ship parcel (B2C) rather than bulk. Parcel cost is higher but matches expected order volume.
- If shifting to bulk: save ~$0.70 per unit in pick-and-pack labor and packaging.
